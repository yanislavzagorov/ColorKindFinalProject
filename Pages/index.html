About Color Vision Deficiency

What is Color Vision Deficiency?

    Color Vision Deficiency (commonly known as “color blindness”) comes in many forms and ranges of severity, but is in essence the same biological disorder. The human eye consists of an array of light-sensitive equipment. Your light-bending lens concentrates the light coming into your eyes through the light-sensing retina to your macula, a point where light is concentrated and later translated into an image in your brain.

    The job of assigning color to these images is divided between three cone-like receptors; one for each red, blue, and green wavelength. When a person is color deficient, this basically means that at least of these cones is misaligned or completely missing, and is not properly displaying the wavelengts that the pupil is taking in.
